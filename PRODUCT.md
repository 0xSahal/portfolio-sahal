# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

International clients evaluating Sahal Shaikh, an independent product engineer, for website
and product work. Usually founders and small teams (often US-based) who found him via Upwork,
Fiverr, LinkedIn, or referral and are deciding whether to trust him with a real budget and
timeline. They arrive skeptical (the web is full of identical freelancer sites) and time-poor.
The job to be done: quickly judge "is this person credible and a safe pair of hands?" and, if
yes, book a call or send a brief.

## Product Purpose

A personal-brand and consulting site whose only goal is to attract and convert high-quality
international clients and book qualified calls. Positioning: "I turn ambiguous ideas into
websites and products that move the business, end to end, from the first call to the final
deploy." Tagline: "Clarity, shipped." Success looks like a qualified visitor booking a call
(Calendly), sending a brief (contact form), or reaching out on WhatsApp.

## Brand Personality

Warm, confident, clear. Speaks like a sharp, friendly operator, not a faceless agency or a
hype-driven freelancer. Business-outcome-first, plain English, no jargon. The writing itself
is part of the proof: clarity is the brand.

## Anti-references

- Generic freelancer and template portfolios. The modal site this must NOT look like.
- AI-generated landing pages and their tells: rounded-border card grids, a pulsing-dot status
  pill, a rounded icon tile above every heading, a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every
  section, identical centered section stacks, gradient text, glassmorphism, hero-metric blocks.
- Cold SaaS aesthetics (purple-blue gradients, Inter, slate-and-navy).
- The saturated editorial-magazine reflex lane (display-serif-italic + mono eyebrows + ruled
  broadsheet columns).

## Design Principles

- **Clarity is the deliverable.** If a visitor is unsure what Sahal does or what to do next,
  the design failed.
- **Proof over promises.** Real client work, real testimonials, real outcomes. Never fabricate.
- **Warm, not corporate.** Feel like a person you'd want in the room, not a vendor RFP.
- **Distinctive, not safe.** Stand out from the flood of identical freelancer sites. It must
  not read as "AI made this." Restraint with intent, never by default.
- **Outcome-first.** Speak to business results and the experience of working together, not the
  tech stack.

## Design Direction (current redesign)

Editorial, type-led, warm. Display face is Bricolage Grotesque (replaced Fraunces, an AI
default). No rounded-border card grids: sections use hairline top-rules, generous whitespace,
and big confident type instead of boxes. No section eyebrows, no icon tiles, no status pill.
Keep the warm palette (paper #f7f5ef, ink #1a1814, honey amber #c5852c).

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Body text at least 4.5:1; large text at least 3:1. Keyboard-navigable with
visible focus rings. Honor prefers-reduced-motion with a crossfade/instant alternative for
every animation. Light and dark themes, both warm.
